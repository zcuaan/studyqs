## Diffusion and Dilution

**Diffusion** is the movement of particles from an area of high concentration to an area of low concentration.

- It happens naturally so no energy is needed.
- When temperature is increased, it will occur faster as particles have more kinetic energy.
- 
**Diffusion in Gas Experiment**
- Using a jar which has a glass plate in the middle to split it in half, fill half a jar with bromine gas (brown), fill the other half of the jar with air.
- Take the glass plate out, the two will diffuse.
- After a while, the colour will be a light coloured gas, showing diffusion.

**Diffusion in Liquids Experiment**
- Put potassium manganate (VII) crystals in a beaker of water.
- After some time, the crystals will diffuse through the water.
- The solution will turn purple.

**Dilution** is adding more solvent (usually water) to a solution

This decreases the concentration of solute particles in the solution.

**Dilution in Liquids Experiment**
- Put potassium manganate (VII) crystals in a beaker of water.
- After some time, the crystals will diffuse through the water.
- The solution will turn purple.
- Add more water and the colour will become lighter, this is dilution.
